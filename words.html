WORDS

For the One Searching at 2AM

You probably didn’t land here by accident.

Maybe you were scrolling. Maybe you weren’t even sure what you were looking for—just that something felt off. Or tender. Or loud. Or gone quiet in a way that scared you.

Maybe you typed something into Google that felt strange to write out.
Like “I’m not broken but I need help.”
Or “something’s changing and I don’t know what.”
Or maybe you didn’t type anything. Maybe you just ended up here.

I’m not going to tell you that this is the answer.
But I will tell you: I’ve been there too.
Not in your exact shape, but in the restlessness.
The ache. The quiet knowing that something needed to shift
long before I had the language to say it.

I’m a therapist. But even that word can feel too small.
What I offer isn’t advice. It isn’t steps or frameworks.
It’s presence. Space. A place to sit with the questions that don’t fit anywhere else.

If something in you is waking up at this hour,
you don’t need to fix it right now.
You just need to know: it’s real. And it’s welcome here.

—Moin
